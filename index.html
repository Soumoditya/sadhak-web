Questions, answered

Everything you might ask.

Is Sadhak free to use?

Yes. Sadhak is free to begin — daily Panchang, the Hindu calendar, nearby temples, the sacred library, aartis and japa are all included at no cost, with no account required to start.

Does Sadhak show an accurate Panchang for my city?

Yes. Sadhak computes tithi, nakshatra, yoga, karana and muhurats from real astronomical calculation for your exact location, sunrise, sunset and timezone — so your daily Hindu Panchang is precise, not a copied almanac table.

Can I find nearby temples and bhandaras?

Yes. Sadhak opens a live, interactive map of Hindu temples around you using your current location, with search, filters and directions — so you can discover and reach temples near you easily.

What does the Hindu calendar include?

The living Hindu calendar highlights festivals, ekadashi, purnima, amavasya and auspicious days, with grooming and activity guidance tuned to the tithi — a practical Hindu festival calendar for the whole year.

Which languages does Sadhak support?

Sadhak is being built for Bharat, with support planned across major Indian languages including Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, Gujarati, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Punjabi, Assamese and Odia.

Is my data private?

Yes. Your practice is yours. Sadhak keeps data minimal and never sells it, and ads (where present) stay out of your reading, chanting and prayer.
